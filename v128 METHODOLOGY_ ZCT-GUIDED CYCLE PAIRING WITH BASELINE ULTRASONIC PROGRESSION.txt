Degradation Ordering: Cycle pairs ordered by mean baseline ultrasonic energy (35-45kHz in quiet segments)

Operational Fingerprints: ZCT temporal patterns distinguish flight phases (startup, cruise, transient, landing) independent of degradation
Cycle Pairing Logic: Files from the same cycle share operational characteristics but may differ in phase
Persistent Damage Signature: Baseline ultrasonic noise floor (measured during quietest moments) captures cumulative structural damage
Monotonic Degradation: Cycle pairs order chronologically as bearing damage accumulates irreversibly
Key Innovation:
Multi-Domain Pairing: First application combining ZCT temporal features with baseline ultrasonic for intelligent file grouping
Operational Pattern Recognition: Uses transient count, pattern regularity, load change indicators, and RPM cyclicity to identify similar flight cycles
Distance-Based Matching: Greedy pairing algorithm minimizes combined distance in ZCT-feature space (60% weight) and degradation space (40% weight)
Two-Level Ordering: Orders cycles chronologically by degradation, then orders files within cycles by operational phase
Cycle Structure Preservation: Maintains physical reality of repeating operational cycles throughout bearing lifetime
