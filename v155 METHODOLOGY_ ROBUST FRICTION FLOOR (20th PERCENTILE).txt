* Feature: 20th Percentile of Rolling RMS Energy ($P_{20}$).
* Frequency Band: 35-45 kHz (Ultrasonic).
* Windowing: 1000 samples (~10ms).
* Ranking: Monotonic ascending sort of the sustained friction floor.
* Incident Files: Fixed at ranks 51, 52, 531.
Physical Rationale:
* Sustained vs. Transient Friction: Stage 1 degradation is characterized by asperity contact (rubbing) that breaks the lubrication film2.
* The Percentile Pivot:
   * Lower Percentiles (5th/10th): Measure the "silence between the rubs." While effective, this can be fooled by sensor dropouts or momentary decoupling, creating false positives for health (e.g., placing damaged files too early).
   * 20th Percentile: Measures the density of the friction events. It represents the "base load" of the noise floor. A file must be quiet for a significant portion of time (80%) to rank highly, effectively filtering out "lucky silence."
Key Innovation:
* Dropout Rejection: By shifting the statistical threshold up to the 20th percentile, the methodology creates a "High-Pass Confidence Filter," ensuring that files ranked early are genuinely healthy rather than just suffering from signal loss.
