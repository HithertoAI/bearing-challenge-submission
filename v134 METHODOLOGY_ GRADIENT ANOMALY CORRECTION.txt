* Baseline Ultrasonic Ordering: Files ordered by 35-45kHz energy in quietest 10% of signal segments
* Gradient Analysis: Calculate baseline change between consecutive ranks in initial ordering
* Anomaly Detection: Identify files where gradient exceeds mean + 2σ threshold
* Targeted Swaps: Reposition anomalous files to minimize local gradient discontinuities

* Smooth Progression Principle: True chronological order should exhibit gradual baseline ultrasonic increase
* Anomaly Hypothesis: Large gradient jumps indicate misplaced files disrupting natural progression

* Monotonic Degradation: Bearing damage accumulates continuously, requiring smooth metric progression

* Anomaly-Targeted Correction: Surgical adjustments rather than accepting raw baseline ordering

* Two-Stage Approach: Initial ordering followed by gradient-driven refinement
